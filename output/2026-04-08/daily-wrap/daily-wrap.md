## The post (copy-paste ready)

𝗪𝗘𝗗𝗡𝗘𝗦𝗗𝗔𝗬 𝗔𝗣𝗥𝗜𝗟 𝟴. Dow closed up 1,312 points, or 2.82%, at 47,897. S&P 500 jumped 2.51% to 6,782.21. Nasdaq added 2.80%. Best day for the Dow since April 2025. One headline did all the work. Trump announced a two-week US-Iran ceasefire, Iran agreed to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, and five weeks of war-premium oil unwound in two hours.

Oil was the main event. WTI crude crashed 17% to $93.42. Brent lost 16% to $91.65. Both benchmarks shed roughly a sixth of their value in a single session, coming straight out of the $110-plus prints the market saw this week. The two-week pause is fragile, but the positioning was so stretched that any relief was going to move fast.

Delta kicked off airline earnings pre-market with a clean beat. Adjusted EPS of $0.64 against $0.61 consensus, revenue of $14.2B against $13.94B, premium ticket revenue up 14% to $5.4B. The stock was already up 8% in pre-market and kept adding, closing the session up roughly 12%. CEO Ed Bastian flagged a $2B Q2 fuel hit and said Delta will pull capacity, which matters less tonight than it did yesterday morning.

The March FOMC meeting minutes hit at 2 PM and told a split story. The vote was 11 to 1 with Governor Stephen Miran the lone dissent for a 25 basis point cut, citing a February payroll print that lost 92,000 jobs and an unemployment rate ticking to 4.4%. Christopher Waller, who dissented with Miran in January, returned to the majority this time. Hawks Lorie Logan and Beth Hammack pushed back on services inflation and $110-plus oil. The catch: today's crude crash just knocked the floor out from under the hawk argument. June cut odds landed at 49% post-release.

Gold rose 2.8% as real yields fell and the dollar softened on the oil crash. Bitcoin gained 4% to join the risk-on party. The 10-year Treasury yield dropped about 10 basis points early, settling lower into the close as the oil-driven inflation fear unwound in real time.

Constellation Brands reported after the bell and gave the one off-note of the day. Comparable EPS of $1.90 beat the $1.71 consensus and revenue of $1.92B beat $1.86B, but the FY27 EPS guide of $11.20 to $11.90 came in well below the $12.37 Street number. Shares fell 2% to $147.26 in extended trading. Modelo keeps gaining share. The guide is what matters tonight.

Tomorrow: Constellation's conference call at 8 AM ET with incoming CEO Nicholas Fink, weekly jobless claims at 8:30 AM, and the first full session under the ceasefire framework. The March CPI print lands Friday at 8:30 AM with FactSet consensus near 3.7% headline and 2.7% core. That print sets the rate path for May.
