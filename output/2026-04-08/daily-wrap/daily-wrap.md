## The post (copy-paste ready)

𝗪𝗘𝗗𝗡𝗘𝗦𝗗𝗔𝗬 𝗔𝗣𝗥𝗜𝗟 𝟴. Dow closed up 1,312 points, or 2.82%, at 47,897. S&P 500 jumped 2.51% to 6,782.21. Nasdaq added 2.80%. One headline did all the work. Trump announced a two-week US-Iran ceasefire, Iran agreed to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, and five weeks of war-premium oil unwound in two hours.

Oil was the main event. WTI crude crashed 17% to $93.42. Brent lost 16% to $91.65. These are the biggest single-session drops in a year, and they come straight out of the $100-plus prints the market saw last week. The two-week pause is fragile, but the positioning was so stretched that any relief was going to move fast.

Delta kicked off airline earnings pre-market with a clean beat. Adjusted EPS of $0.64 against $0.61 consensus, revenue of $14.2B against $13.94B, premium ticket revenue up 14%. The stock was already up 8% in pre-market and kept adding, closing the session up roughly 12%. CEO Ed Bastian flagged a $2B Q2 fuel hit and said Delta will pull capacity, which matters less tonight than it did yesterday morning.

The March FOMC meeting minutes hit at 2 PM and told a split story. Governor Stephen Miran and Christopher Waller dissented in favor of a 25 basis point cut, citing the weak 92,000 February payroll print. Lorie Logan and Beth Hammack pushed back on services inflation. The catch: the hawks built their case on $100 oil, and today's crude crash just knocked the floor out from under that argument. June cut odds on CME FedWatch landed at 49% post-minutes.

Gold rose 2.8% as real yields fell and the dollar softened on the oil crash. Bitcoin gained 4% to join the risk-on party. The 10-year Treasury yield dropped about 10 basis points early, settling lower into the close as the oil-driven inflation fear unwound in real time.

Constellation Brands reported after the bell and gave the one off-note of the day. Comparable EPS of $1.90 beat the $1.71 consensus and revenue of $1.92B beat $1.86B, but the FY27 EPS guide of $11.20 to $11.90 came in well below the $12.37 Street number. Shares fell 2% to $147.26 in extended trading. Modelo keeps gaining share. The guide is what matters tonight.

Tomorrow: Constellation's conference call at 8 AM ET with incoming CEO Nicholas Fink, weekly jobless claims at 8:30 AM, and the first full session under the ceasefire framework. The March CPI print lands Friday at 8:30 AM and will set the rate-cut path for May.
