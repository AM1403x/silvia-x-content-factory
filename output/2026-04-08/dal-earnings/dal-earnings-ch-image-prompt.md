# DAL Earnings Card — Calvin & Hobbes Illustration Prompt

Paste the block below into ChatGPT-4o image generation, DALL-E 3, or Midjourney. Landscape orientation, roughly 3:2 aspect ratio.

For the base style reference, see [triplex/prompts/ch_image_style_guide.md](../../../triplex/prompts/ch_image_style_guide.md).

## Prompt

Illustrate in the style of Bill Watterson's Calvin and Hobbes. Hand-drawn ink outlines with visible pen strokes, soft watercolor fills with slight edge bleed, off-white cream paper background with a light watercolor wash texture. Flat watercolor coloring with minimal shading, just enough to give volume. No photographic realism, no digital gradients, no 3D effects, no drop shadows, no corporate logos.

Scene: A young adult man in his late 20s, messy short brown hair, clean-shaven with expressive eyebrows. He wears a white long-sleeve dress shirt with the sleeves rolled up to mid-forearm, a navy blue tie loose around his neck, and dark trousers. He is standing beside his wooden office desk with both arms raised high overhead in a victorious celebration pose, fists clenched. His face shows a huge open-mouthed grin, eyes squeezed shut in pure joy. His posture is energetic, as if he just heard the best news of the morning.

On the desk to his left: a computer monitor displaying a bright green ascending stock chart line climbing steeply from lower-left to upper-right, with the plain text "DAL" in the corner of the screen. Next to the monitor sits a simple white paper airplane, a nod to Delta's airline business. A ceramic coffee mug with a wisp of steam curling up sits beside the monitor.

On the floor behind him: a wooden oil barrel tipped over on its side, with a small puddle of dark liquid (crude oil) spreading out and two or three droplets frozen mid-flight as if the barrel just fell. This represents the Iran ceasefire crashing oil prices the same morning Delta reported — two tailwinds at once.

Soft watercolor palette: white cream paper background with gentle wash texture, white shirt, navy blue tie as the only cool color on the character, brown messy hair, warm wooden desk, the bright green monitor chart as the strongest color anchor in the frame, muted dark brown oil barrel and spill. Visible ink outlines on every element, slightly uneven and hand-drawn.

Landscape aspect ratio, roughly 3:2 or 16:10. The character fills about 40% of the frame height, positioned slightly right of center. The monitor and paper airplane occupy the left third. The tipped oil barrel is in the lower-right corner of the frame, behind and to the right of the character.

Mood: pure celebration. Two tailwinds at once — a clean Q1 earnings beat and a 17% oil crash from the ceasefire. The viewer should immediately feel the "double-win morning" energy through the character's body language alone.

Hard rules:
- No photographic realism
- No digital gradients, no drop shadows, no 3D rendering
- No real Delta Air Lines logo — the monitor shows only the plain letters "DAL"
- No text on the image except "DAL" on the monitor
- No stock photography elements
- Character must feel hand-drawn with watercolor, not vector art
- The oil barrel must be clearly identifiable as an oil drum (ridged metal cylinder), tipped on its side with visible spill

- Mood: double-tailwind celebration
- Character expression: ecstatic open-mouthed grin, eyes squeezed shut, both fists raised

## Regeneration notes

- If the model adds a real Delta logo, add: "NO Delta Air Lines logo, NO real airline branding. Only the plain letters DAL on the monitor."
- If the oil barrel looks like a wine barrel or coffee can, add: "The oil barrel is a modern steel industrial drum, ridged sides, clearly labeled or recognizable as petroleum."
- If the character looks too generic or corporate, add: "Messy Watterson-style brown hair, slightly unkempt, expressive Bill Watterson character drawing, not a clean vector office worker."
