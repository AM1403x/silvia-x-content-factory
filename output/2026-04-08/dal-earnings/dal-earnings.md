## The post (copy-paste ready)

𝗗𝗔𝗟 Q1 BEAT. EPS $0.45 vs $0.39 est. Revenue $14.1B vs $13.8B. Stock up 3.2% pre-market. The quiet story: premium cabin revenue grew 12% while main cabin went flat. That gap is the whole thesis.

Three things that mattered.

1. Premium revenue hit $5.8B this quarter, a record for the March period. Delta is capturing the higher-end traveler Marriott and AmEx keep talking about, and the margin gap between front cabin and back cabin has never been wider.

2. International was up 8% year over year, with transatlantic leading. Delta added capacity to Rome, Athens, and Nice ahead of the summer peak. That is a bet on the same European travel boom driving Booking and Expedia.

3. Jet fuel averaged $2.71 per gallon, up 5% from a year ago. Delta absorbed it because cost per seat mile excluding fuel only rose 1.4%. That is the operational tightness you want to see in a quarter where the top line could have carried the story alone.

Forward guidance: Q2 EPS of $2.00 to $2.30, a narrower band than last year's sprawling range, and June bookings are running 4% ahead of 2025. Management called the current demand environment "resilient at the top, soft at the bottom."

If you own DAL: the premium-heavy positioning is doing exactly what you paid for. Watch main cabin yield in the Q2 report. If that stays flat while premium holds 10%+ growth, the multiple re-rating case gets real.
