# FOMC Minutes Card — Calvin & Hobbes Illustration Prompt

Paste the block below into ChatGPT-4o image generation, DALL-E 3, or Midjourney. Landscape orientation, roughly 3:2 aspect ratio.

For the base style reference, see [triplex/prompts/ch_image_style_guide.md](../../../triplex/prompts/ch_image_style_guide.md).

---

## Prompt

Illustrate in the style of Bill Watterson's Calvin and Hobbes. Hand-drawn ink outlines with visible pen strokes, soft watercolor fills with slight edge bleed, off-white cream paper background with a light watercolor wash texture. Flat watercolor coloring with minimal shading, just enough to give volume. No photographic realism, no digital gradients, no 3D effects, no drop shadows, no corporate logos.

Scene: A formal Federal Reserve boardroom interior. A large round polished wooden conference table dominates the center of the frame. Around the table, eleven empty wooden chairs stand upright in neat, evenly-spaced positions. But on the near-right side of the round table, one twelfth chair is knocked over, tipped back onto the floor at an angle, clearly out of place. This one knocked-over chair represents Governor Stephen Miran's lone dissent in the 11-1 FOMC vote.

Our recurring young adult character stands behind the table on the viewer's right side. He has messy short brown hair, wears a white long-sleeve dress shirt, a dark charcoal gray tie (serious Fed tone), and dark trousers. In both hands he is holding up a manila document folder with the plain text "FOMC MINUTES" written on the cover in hand-drawn letters. He is reading the document with one eyebrow raised skeptically and a small wry smirk on his face — the face of someone who just realized something funny.

On the wall behind the round table: a framed painting of the Federal Reserve's Eccles Building facade — recognizable neoclassical architecture with tall columns and a central stone entrance, rendered in simple watercolor with hand-drawn lines. The frame is simple dark wood.

On a small side table to the viewer's left, at roughly knee height: a wooden oil barrel tipped over on its side, with a small puddle of dark crude oil spreading on the floor. A few oil droplets frozen mid-air next to the spill. This represents the oil crash that same day, which undercut the hawks' main argument about $110 oil fueling inflation.

Soft watercolor palette: cream paper background, warm polished wooden round table, muted brown chairs (the knocked-over one the same color but clearly on its side), white shirt, charcoal gray tie on the character, brown hair, the Eccles Building painting in warm browns and stone grays, dark brown oil barrel and spill. The lighting should feel like a quiet moment after a meeting adjourned.

Landscape aspect ratio, roughly 3:2 or 16:10. The round conference table with its eleven upright chairs and one tipped chair fills the foreground and middle of the frame. The character stands in the upper-right, holding up the FOMC MINUTES document. The Eccles Building painting hangs in the upper-center on the wall. The oil barrel sits in the lower-left of the frame for visual balance.

Mood: understated dry irony. The hawks held the line 11-1 in an already-serious meeting, and on the same day the minutes hit the market, the expensive-oil argument that grounded their stance literally spilled onto the floor. Classic Watterson visual wit: the story is told by what is on the floor, not by anyone saying anything.

Hard rules:
- No photographic realism
- No digital gradients, no drop shadows, no 3D rendering
- No real Federal Reserve seal, no US flag, no specific political figures
- No text on the image except "FOMC MINUTES" on the document cover
- Character must feel hand-drawn with watercolor, not vector art
- The tipped chair must be clearly one of the twelve around the table, matching the other eleven in design, just on its side
- The Eccles Building painting should be recognizable as a neoclassical government building without any signage

## Post-specific notes

- Tie color: DARK CHARCOAL GRAY (Fed-serious tone)
- Key props: round table with 11 upright chairs + 1 tipped chair (the 11-1 vote), FOMC MINUTES document, Eccles Building wall painting, tipped oil barrel (undercut hawk argument)
- Mood: understated dry irony
- Character expression: raised eyebrow, wry smirk, "huh" face while reading the document

## Regeneration notes

- If the model draws fewer than 12 chairs, add: "Exactly twelve chairs around the round table: eleven upright in their positions, one knocked over on its side on the floor. Count them."
- If the Eccles Building looks like a White House or Capitol, add: "The wall painting shows the Federal Reserve Eccles Building — a white neoclassical building with six tall columns across the front, a central stone entrance, and a flat roofline. Not the Capitol dome, not the White House."
- If the document cover is blank, add: "The document cover has the plain hand-drawn text 'FOMC MINUTES' clearly visible at the top."
- If the oil barrel is misinterpreted as a bucket or tank, add: "The oil barrel is a standard steel industrial drum — ridged cylindrical sides, painted brown or black — tipped on its side with crude oil spilling out."
