## Scene prompt

A formal Federal Reserve boardroom interior. A large round polished wooden conference table dominates the center of the frame. Around the table, eleven empty wooden chairs stand upright in neat, evenly-spaced positions. But on the near-right side of the round table, one twelfth chair is knocked over, tipped back onto the floor at an angle, clearly out of place. This one knocked-over chair represents Governor Stephen Miran's lone dissent in the 11-1 FOMC vote.

The recurring young adult character stands behind the table on the viewer's right side. He has messy short brown hair, wears a white long-sleeve dress shirt, a dark charcoal gray tie (serious Fed tone), and dark trousers. In both hands he is holding up a manila document folder with the plain text "FOMC MINUTES" written on the cover. He is reading the document with one eyebrow raised skeptically and a small wry smirk on his face, the face of someone who just realized something funny.

On the wall behind the round table: a framed painting of the Federal Reserve's Eccles Building facade, recognizable neoclassical architecture with tall columns and a central stone entrance, rendered simply. The frame is simple dark wood.

On a small side table to the viewer's left, at roughly knee height: a wooden oil barrel tipped over on its side, with a small puddle of dark crude oil spreading on the floor. A few oil droplets frozen mid-air next to the spill. This represents the oil crash that same day, which undercut the hawks' main argument about $110 oil fueling inflation.

Landscape aspect ratio, roughly 3:2 or 16:10. The round conference table with its eleven upright chairs and one tipped chair fills the foreground and middle of the frame. The character stands in the upper-right, holding up the FOMC MINUTES document. The Eccles Building painting hangs in the upper-center on the wall. The oil barrel sits in the lower-left of the frame for visual balance.

Mood: understated dry irony. The hawks held the line 11-1 in an already-serious meeting, and on the same day the minutes hit the market, the expensive-oil argument that grounded their stance literally spilled onto the floor. Visual wit: the story is told by what is on the floor, not by anyone saying anything.

Hard rules:
- No real Federal Reserve seal, no US flag, no specific political figures
- No text on the image except "FOMC MINUTES" on the document cover
- No photographic realism
- The tipped chair must be clearly one of the twelve around the table, matching the other eleven in design, just on its side
- The Eccles Building painting should be recognizable as a neoclassical government building without any signage
