## The post (copy-paste ready)

𝗙𝗢𝗠𝗖 𝗠𝗜𝗡𝗨𝗧𝗘𝗦 HAWKISH. The March 17-18 vote was 11 to 1, with Governor Stephen Miran the lone dissent for a 25 basis point cut. Christopher Waller, who dissented with Miran in January, voted to hold this time. June cut odds landed at 49% on CME FedWatch after the release.

Miran's reasoning was the February payroll shock. The US economy lost 92,000 jobs that month, the biggest drop in four months against a 50,000 gain consensus, and unemployment ticked to 4.4%. Miran called the labor market "bending, if not yet breaking," arguing that waiting for more data meant letting it break. A later revision cut the February number to a loss of 133,000 and only strengthened his case.

The hawks still won. Lorie Logan and Beth Hammack flagged services inflation above 4% and oil punching through $110 a barrel as reasons to hold. Chair Powell struck a "mildly hawkish" tone, downplaying the March dot plot while leaving the door open to later cuts. The fed funds target stayed at 3.50% to 3.75%.

Here is the twist. The oil scenario the hawks used as cover just died. WTI crashed 17% today to $93.42 on the Trump ceasefire headline, exactly during the two hours the minutes were being digested. The hawkish case built on $110-plus oil is now built on $93 oil and falling. The dissent looks prescient by one month.

What to watch next: March CPI at 8:30 AM on Friday, April 10. FactSet consensus sits at 3.7% headline year over year with core closer to 2.7%. A cool print on top of crashed oil and June cut odds go back above 60% by Monday.

The take: the hawks wrote their case in ink and the ink is already running.
