## The post (copy-paste ready)

𝗦𝗧𝗭 (Constellation Brands) Q4 BEAT, GUIDE CUT. Comparable EPS $1.90 vs $1.71 est. Revenue $1.92B vs $1.86B est. Reported EPS $1.16 under GAAP. Stock fell 2% to $147.26 after hours on an FY27 forecast of $11.20 to $11.90 against a $12.37 Street number. Beer wins, guidance loses.

Three things that mattered.

1. Modelo Especial held its position as the top US beer brand by dollar sales and ranked fourth in dollar share gains for the quarter. Constellation holds six of the top fifteen share gainers across the full beer category for the fiscal year. The brand machine is still working.

2. Pacifico depletions grew 15% in the quarter with Victoria also posting strong double-digit growth. The premium tier is where Constellation is winning. The mainstream beer picture, and the broader wine and spirits book, is where the problems live.

3. The FY27 guide is why the stock is red after hours. $11.20 to $11.90 against a $12.37 consensus implies beer growth is slower than the Street had modeled. That is the whole story of the after-hours print.

Forward look: the conference call lands April 9 at 8 AM ET with incoming CEO Nicholas Fink making his debut alongside Bill Newlands and CFO Garth Hankinson. The board raised the dividend 1% to $1.03 a share in a separate release, a small signal the company is not panicking about cash.

If you own STZ: Modelo is still executing. The Street had the beer growth curve tighter than Constellation will now guide. Watch the April 9 call for Hispanic consumer trends, premium pricing, and any hint on wine divestitures. If Fink sounds decisive, the $147 level holds.
