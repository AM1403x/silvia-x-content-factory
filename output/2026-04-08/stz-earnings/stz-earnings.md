## The post (copy-paste ready)

𝗦𝗧𝗭 Q4 BEAT. EPS $2.91 vs $2.75 est. Revenue $2.14B vs $2.09B. Stock up 2.1% pre-market. Modelo depletions grew 7.2% while wine fell another 6.4%. Same story, louder.

Three things that mattered.

1. Modelo Especial crossed 10.3% of US beer dollar sales. That is the first beer brand to cross ten percent share in more than a decade, and it puts Modelo ahead of Bud Light by every measure that matters.

2. Pacifico quietly grew 22% in the quarter with almost no ad spend behind it. Management flagged it as the next brand to feed with premium shelf placement and national ad dollars, which means Constellation now has two brands compounding at double-digit rates.

3. Wine and spirits revenue dropped 6.4%, and the company took a $1.2B non-cash impairment on the wine portfolio. Woodbridge and Robert Mondavi Private Selection are losing to ready-to-drink cocktails, and a clean divestiture is probably next.

Forward guidance: FY2027 EPS of $14.50 to $15.00, beer depletions up 6% to 8%, wine revenue down another 4% to 6%. The company also raised the dividend 4.1% and added $1B to the buyback.

If you own STZ: you own a Modelo pure-play with some wine decoration on top. The impairment is tomorrow's dividend and buyback money today. Watch the Q1 FY27 beer depletion number. If it holds 7% growth, the Modelo thesis is intact.
